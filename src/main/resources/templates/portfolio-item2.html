The Beauty of Chinese Food

Chinese Food:
Chinese cuisine is an important constituent part of Chinese culture. Rather than using forks, chopsticks are the major eating utensils for Chinese dishes that can be used to pick up the food. The most essential characteristics of Chinese food comprise of color, aroma, and taste.

Diversified Colors:
A dish with diversified colors arouse gourmets� appetite. In fact, Chinese dishes focus on the aesthetic appearance for many years. In order to achieve this, by adding one or two different ingredient colors for the decoration purpose is one of the method to impress people.

Aromatic Flavors:
A dish with aromatic flavor is one of the crucial factor for Chinese cuisine. To make the dishes fragrant in flavor, Chinese chefs would used ash seeds, cinnamon, as well as some sources to dispel the smell for the particular stinks.

Fantastic Tastes:
The tastes of Chinese cuisine consist of five different classes including sweet, sour, bitter and hot. Each taste represents different eating habits of the people, such as South - sweet, East - Hot and West - Sour. Those southern residence of China like to add more sugar than other regions. For example, Jiangsu Cuisine one of the 'Eight Cuisines' is representative of 'South-Sweet�. Whereas, the eastern residence of China like to add more hot and chill than than other regions, such as Sichuan and Chongqing.
